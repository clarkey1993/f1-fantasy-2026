## 4. What Can Produce Exactly ±1

Only these can change the total by 1:

1. **Lap points:** 1 lap difference (rules: 1 pt per lap).
2. **Grid points:** 1 grid position (21 − pos).
3. **Gain points:** 1 position gained.
4. **Finish points:** e.g. P10 = 1 pt.
5. **Constructor points:** e.g. 10th = 1 pt or finisher bonus 10.

## 6. Final Verdict

**1. Remaining code bug**

- No evidence of an app bug. Lap data matches FastF1 (`results.Laps` and `session.laps` count); grid, gain, finish, and constructor logic match the rules and are internally consistent.

**2. Consistent scorer rule/source difference**

- The ±1 pattern is best explained by a **lap count source/rule difference**.
- App: FastF1 `results.Laps` (or `session.laps` count when Laps=0).
- Manual scorers: likely another source (e.g. FIA timing, different API) that can differ by 1 lap for lapped finishers.
- All affected line-ups include Franco Colapinto (56 laps); he is the strongest candidate for the systematic ±1.
- Without the manual scorers’ per-driver breakdown, the exact pick cannot be proven.

**3. Recommendation**

- Treat the app as correct under the current rules and FastF1 data.
- If league policy is to follow manual scorers, align the app’s lap source with theirs (or document the chosen source in the rules).
